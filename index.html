About Lael Wilcox

Lael Wilcox is an ultra-endurance bicycle racer. Born on July 28, 1987, Lael only started riding at 20 years old as a way to commute. This soon turned into an 11-year journey around the world. Lael first became interested in racing in 2014 when she was in Israel and entered the Holyland Challenge, a 1000-mile unsupported race across the country. She was both the youngest rider and the only woman. She led the race by 25 miles the first day, and although she did not win the race, she was firmly focused on endurance racing.

Races

In 2015, Lael set the Tour Divide's women's course record on an individual time trial. In 2016, She won the Trans Am Bike Race and was the first American to win it. In winter 2015 she co-developed the Baja Divide route and in 2017, she also set the overall course record with her time on the Baja Divide route, a route which runs from San Diego, California through San Jose del Cabo and on to La Paz, Mexico.
